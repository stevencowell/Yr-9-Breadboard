Bread Board Unit

This unit provides students with the opportunity to gain basic hand skills and confidence in working with timber. Over 10 weeks, students will learn to safely use a range of tools and equipment, master joinery techniques, and document their processes in line with key Industrial Technology outcomes.

Summary & Duration

Duration: 10 Weeks (40-minute lessons, 4 per week)
Focus: Workshop safety, marking & measuring, cutting, joinery, finishing, self-evaluation, and project presentation.

Unit Overview

This “Bread Board” unit (Stage 5) focuses on building practical woodworking skills alongside an understanding of safety and design principles. Students use hand, power, and machine tools to construct a bread board while meeting both theory (5%) and practical (13%) assessment criteria.

Learning Outcomes & Assessment

  • IND51: Manage risks and WHS issues.
  • IND52: Apply design principles to projects.
  • IND53: Demonstrate effective use of tools.
  • IND54: Justify material selection.
  • IND55: Communicate project ideas effectively.
  • IND56: Engage in collaborative work practices.
  • IND57: Transfer skills across projects.
  • IND58: Evaluate project quality on multiple criteria.
  • IND59: Analyse new timber technologies.
  • IND510: Assess the societal and environmental impact of technology.

Assessment: Practical – 13% | Theory – 5%

Weekly Breakdown

Week 1: Workshop Introduction, Marking & Measuring Exercise

Content: Introduction to workshop safety, proper use of marking and measuring tools, and understanding WHS signage.

Theory: Onguard topics, General Workshop Safety, and Workplace Safety Signs discussions.

Practical: Demonstration of marking out lengths with hand tools and a guided workshop tour.

Resources: Design Brief, Safety Guidelines, Google Classroom – Week 1.

Week 2: Mark, Cut & Plane Breadboard Sides; Onguard Safety Tests

Content: Techniques for marking, cutting, and planing timber edges with an emphasis on safety.

Theory: Onguard topics, discussions on Woodworking Handtools, and Workplace Safety Signs.

Practical: Hands-on practice with jack plane, try square, marking gauge, and tenon saw.

Resources: SOP Disc Sander, Google Classroom – Week 2.

Week 3: Biscuit Join Sides & Cut Ends

Content: Learn biscuit joining techniques and cutting accurate board ends; focus on grain direction and joint design.

Theory: Discussions on timber grain, board layout, and various joint types.

Practical: Demonstration and practice using a Biscuit Jointer, Pedestal Drill, and disc sander.

Resources: SOP Biscuit Cutter, Google Classroom – Week 3.

Week 4: Shaping Ends & Laser Cutter Demonstration

Content: Introduction to shaping timber using a jigsaw and exploring laser engraving for decorative patterns.

Theory: Discussions on design options for ends and handles; Laser Cutter overview.

Practical: Teacher demonstration of laser cutter and jigsaw; students sketch design ideas.

Resources: SOP Disc Sander, SOP Pedestal Drill, Google Classroom – Week 4.

Week 5: Completing Laser-Cut Patterns & Biscuit Join Ends

Content: Finalise laser-cut patterns and join patterned ends to the main board using adhesive and clamps.

Theory: Discussion on joining techniques and safe workshop practices.

Practical: Dry fitting using sash cramps and demonstration of glue and clamping.

Resources: Joinery Research materials, Google Classroom – Week 5.

Week 6: Router Demonstration & Safety Tests

Content: Learn how to safely use a router to cut profiles and implement risk management procedures.

Theory: Onguard topics, discussions on router bits and design options.

Practical: Demonstration of router usage and creation of a router profile sheet.

Resources: Router Profile Sheet, Google Classroom – Week 6.

Week 7: Finishing Patterns, Sanding & Preparation

Content: Complete decorative patterns, sand, and prep surfaces for finishing.

Theory: Discussions on sanding techniques, abrasive materials, and timber seasoning processes.

Practical: Demonstration of sanding techniques and surface preparation.

Resources: Research on Timber Seasoning, Google Classroom – Week 7.

Week 8: Oil Timber Finish & SWMS Completion

Content: Apply an oil finish (e.g. Danish Oil) and complete a Safe Work Method Statement (SWMS).

Theory: Discussions on finishing materials, renewable resources, and sustainability in timber.

Practical: Demonstration of oil finishing techniques.

Resources: Research on Environmental Sustainability, Google Classroom – Week 8.

Week 9: Self-Evaluation

Content: Reflect on and evaluate the completed project, documenting processes and outcomes.

Theory: Discussion on self-evaluation and record-keeping in project management.

Practical: Creation of a self-evaluation report and update of the SWMS.

Resources: Self-Evaluation Guidelines, Google Classroom – Week 9.

Week 10: Project Presentation & Workshop Maintenance

Content: Final project presentation with a comprehensive project booklet and discussion on workshop maintenance.

Theory: Discussion on project documentation, maintenance practices, and environmental impact.

Practical: Final project demonstration and workshop maintenance cleanup.

Resources: Google Classroom – Week 10.
